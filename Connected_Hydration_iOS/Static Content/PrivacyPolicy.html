How We Handle Your Personal Information


When you create a user account for certain Epicore Biosystems Services, we ask for some personal information (for example your name, email address, biological sex at birth, and age). We need this information in order to provide you with a personalized experience with our Services. For example, we use your height, weight and biological sex at birth information to compute your whole body sweat loss which determines your rehydration recommendation. If you give us false information about your height, weight, and biological sex at birth, our algorithms will not calculate correct results for you, and we therefore trust that all the information you provide are accurate.

Our sensors collect sweat loss volume and sweat electrolyte loss, temperature, and motion to generate a hydration recommendation. When you use your phone to log data, GPS coordinates are collected. This happens only if you give consent specifically for geolocation and when you use the Epicore Biosystems app to actively log information to allow for analysis of pertinent weather information and heat wave warnings.

If you subscribe to Epicore Biosystems’ newsletter, we will collect your email address. We only process your personal information for the purposes listed in this Privacy Policy and for the service-specific purposes on which you can find more information by clicking on the links provided. Using User Data for Research and Development


Epicore Biosystems focuses on personalized hydration and metabolic health management solutions. We have in-house experts specializing in material science, microfluidics, bioelectronics, chemistry, physiology, sports training, health and data science, and we carry out studies pertaining to wearable technologies, sports training, heat illness and nutrition on a regular basis.

Types of Research

Epicore Biosystems continuously studies human physiology and hydration to develop new technologies, products and services but also improve on existing ones. New algorithms are being developed and tested, and customer mass data is used to, for example, find anomalies in the features or to find devices that may not be working properly. These results are not shared outside of the company. They only support the in-house development work.

Some of the research is performed in cooperation with third-party research partners. The aim of research collaboration is to produce a scientific publication of the results. In these instances, the research work is conducted according to research study protocols and ethical requirements imposed on the study. In these studies, no customer data is given to the third-party research partners, but instead all data is processed within Epicore Biosystems and by our research team. If any customer personal data is used in the research, customers are asked to review a separate informed consent to use their personal data for the specific research project.

Marketing research aims to better understand users of Epicore Biosystems’ devices and services, inform how the devices are being used in the field, and gauge what new features customers may want in future products. Marketing research is performed by analyzing data from users who have given their consent for marketing purposes.

Connected Hydration Worker Safety Cloud and App


Epicore Biosystems’ Connected Hydration Worker Safety Cloud (the “Cloud”) is the associated cloud-based worker safety service that helps workers proactively hydrate and effectively recover during their work shift. The Cloud is connected to a weather reporting service to provide workers with real-time alerts. The Cloud also analyzes sweat loss, water and electrolyte intake, and temperature to provide recommendations and information to keep you physically and cognitively safe. Information is conveyed to you via the mobile app.

Before you can start using the Cloud, you must create a user account. You have the option to use your Connected Hydration device in a stand-alone mode with limited functionality (such as receiving real-time hydration recommendations); however, any historical data, trend analysis, proactive hydration recommendations based on weather would not be enabled. We will ask you for your consent to share anonymized data collected from you with your employer or partnering enterprise. By providing your consent, you grant Epicore Biosystems permission to handle your data exclusively for the specified purposes and nothing else. If you do consent, your anonymized data will be aggregated into anonymized group statistics, such as the number of bottles of water workers drink a given week, or the number of dehydration alarms of all workers triggered during a given month, which Epicore Biosystems may share with your employer or partner enterprise. Epicore Biosystems does not share personal information (email, height, weight, biological sex at birth, and age) or identifiable individual sensor data with your employer or enterprise partners.
